Chapter 16 & 17 Questions


Chapter 16:

1. What is the difference between givers and takers?

2. How can you tell if someone is more of one than the other?

3. How might refusing to receive from others be a form of selfishness?

4. What does it look like to find a person of respect versus a person who is "incomplete?"
"The kind of respect we're talking about is a respect engendered from a person's character and actions, not position [as a spouse]." (208)
"Marriage doesn't solve emptiness; it exposes it." (211)

5. How does knowing our identity in Christ allow us to love others well, even when it risks their disapproval?


Chapter 17:

1. What does it mean to live life as "mission first" and everything else second, including marriage?

"A happy marriage is [not] the primary goal of [Christian] life. Glorifying God is." (221)

2. How does "mission first" establish the foundation we need to walk through difficult circumstances with a future spouse?
Matthew 6:33 Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness and all these thing will be added unto you.
Matthew 28:20b "'teaching them to observe all things that I have commanded you; and lo, I am with you always, even to the end of the age.' Amen."

3. How can you properly evaluate someone as a potential marriage partner without tearing that person apart?

4. How do you keep that balance between recognizing someone's strengths while also seriously considering weaknesses?
