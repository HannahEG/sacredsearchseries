Chapter 11, 12, & 13 Questions




Chapter 11: Making a Marriage

1. What did you find interesting from the chapter "making a marriage?"

2. When talking about humility what does it mean to be more concerned with light and truth than with being right? What about pride? What about serving?

(Mark not to be served but to serve
James God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble)

3. Why is unforgiveness so detrimental to the survival of a marriage? How can you tell if the person you want to marry is capable of forgiving?

4. What does healthy conflict resolution look like? What about unhealthy conflict resolution?


Chapter 12: Gender Roles
1. What are some likely problems that will arise if a complementarian marries an egalitarian, or vice versa?

2. How might these views affect the way you raise and train your children?


Chapter 13: Compatibility
1. To what degree do people change after they get married? What is the problem with marrying someone for who they might become as opposed to who they are?

2. How important is it to marry someone you not only love but also like and respect? Why?

3. What areas of compatibility might be unwise to compromise on simply because you have strong feelings for someone?

4. If someone starts making compromises on important issues just to keep a relationship together, how can they work through these concerns in a responsible way?
