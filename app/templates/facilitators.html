Test Questions


Chapter 4:

1. Why do you think the same women who, as girlfriends, often defended their boyfriends, now, as wives,
often complain about the same men who have become their husbands? What is going on here?

- Acts 6:3 Choose men who are known to be full of the Spirit and wisdom.

- 1 Peter 5:8 Be sober-minded; be watchful. Your adversary the devil prowls around like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour.

2. Given that men are biologically predisposed to be impressed with physical beauty,
how can they base the decision to marry on a woman's qualities that will outlast physical appearance? Is this a realistic question to ask?

- 1 Peter 5:8 Be sober-minded; be watchful. Your adversary the devil prowls around like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour.

- Proverbs 31:30 Charm is deceitful and beauty is passing, But a woman who fears the Lord, she shall be praised.

- Proverbs 12:4 An excellent wife is the crown of her husband, But she who causes shame is like rottenness in his bones.

3. What is the difference between self-interest and selfishness? How is choosing wisely and
acting wisely good for our self interest but not necessarily an act of selfishness? (pg 51)

-Looking for someone who can walk with me toward God. "It's not selfish to choose wisely in marriage; it's being a good steward of the one life God has given you."

- C.S. Lewis quote: "Our Lord finds out desires not too strong, but too weak. We are half-hearted creatures, fooling about with drink, sex, and ambition, when infinite joy is offered us, like an ignorant child who wants to go on making mud pies in a slum because he cannot imagine what is meant by the offer of a holiday at the sea. We are far too easily pleased."
Chapter 8:

1. What does the Bible say about sex and marriage?
Look up verses and discuss (1 Corinthians 6:15-20, 1 Corin 7:36-38, 1 Thess. 4:3-7, Hebrews 13:4) pg 84

- God made it, God made us to be sexual beings, God made sex ONLY for the confines of marriage.
- For some call to be holy is veiled call to be married pg 89

2. How can someone be motivated by sexual desire but not ruled by it when actively pursuing marriage?
To what extent should this be a consideration for women and to what extent a consideration for men? pg 84

- 1 Corinthians 7:9 but if they cannot exercise self-control, let them marry. For it is better to marry than to burn with passion.
- Proverbs 5:18-19 Let your fountain be blessed, And rejoice with the wife of your youth. As a loving deer and a graceful doe, Let her breasts satisfy you at all times; And always be enraptured with her love.

3. Do you think it is wise to marry someone you highly respect and enjoy being around, but for whom you don't have intense feelings of infatuation?

4. How important is it to you to be "financially secure" before you get married? How can this be balanced against other issues?
