Chapter 2 & 3 Questions


Chapter 2:
1. What role does our entertainment play in determining what people think about the phrase "falling in love?"
To what extent have you personally been impacted by this perspective both currently and in the past?

2. Our culture tells us that happiness, chemistry, intense feelings, and general compatibility
are the most important aspects to find in a lasting relationship.
How does Matthew 6:33 contradict this premise, and again why is it important?
What does it look like?

Chapter 3:
1. Infatuation can be blinding, misleading, or potentially even dangerous.
Have you ever experienced infatuation and how long did it last for you?
What are some clues that infatuation might be setting in?

2. Is it possible to think logically, make good decisions, while feeling "all the fuzzies" of infatuation?
What does it look like to make good decisions and mitigate the "idealization" that comes with infatuation?
Look up the following verses and discuss: Proverbs 11:14, Proverbs 12:15, James 3:2, 1 Thessalonians 5:17

3. What does it look like to be on the opposite side of infatuation as a friend or person acting as a source of accountability?
What are some difficulties or questions you might have when stepping into a role of accountability for someone else?
How many sources of accountability should we have?

4. As Christians we have the Holy Spirit to help us control our thoughts, emotions, and feelings.
What steps or actions can you take, now, to ensure that the Holy Spirit ultimately guides and interprets your emotions/feelings for you?
Practically, why is it important to have a "reasonably accurate" view of someone?
Look up these verses: James 1:5, Psalm 1:2-3, Proverbs 3:6, Jeremiah 17:9

Psalms 106:13-15: A Warning for when we operate without God's council.
