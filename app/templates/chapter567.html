Chapters 5, 6, & 7 Questions


Chapter 5:
1. How does completeness/whole-ness in Christ impact the idea of a finding a "soul mate", and what is the difference between desperation and security while looking for a spouse?

2. If we want to honor God in the face of temptation, what is the difference between flippancy and intentionality?
(*Being flippant with sin is not a reason to get married. Marriage doesn't fix sin. However, for those not called to singleness, Paul says it is better to be married than to burn with passion.)

3.Biblically, what does sacrificial love look like (Verses), and how can we apply sacrificial love to everyday interactions and behaviors now?

Chapter 6:
1. What are some reasons the example of Rebekah and Isaac, David and Bathsheba, or Mary and Joseph are not likely templates for most Christians to follow?

2. Rather than emotional or spiritual shortcuts, what kind of character or confirmation should we seek - for our own lives and in a potential spouse's life?

Chapter 7:
1. What does it look like to "make an effort?" How is this different from acting desperately?

2. How big a role has prayer played in your own pursuit for a potential spouse?
