Chapter 9 & 10 Questions


Chapter 9:

1. Which of the 12 styles described best fit the kind of marriage you imagined having? Or which aspects of these "styles" describe your ideal relationship?

2. What is the significance of honestly and intentionally determining my own and my potential/current partner's marriage assumptions? How important, or is it important, to agree on these aspects?

3. What are your thoughts about the statement that breaking off a relationship when these considerations don't or can't align is "the most loving thing you can do?" (pg 113)


Chapter 10:
1. How would you rank the importance of the four aspects described in this chapter: character, parenting potential, extended family, and spiritual humility? What are the values of each when making potential marriage or relationship decision?

(FACILITATORS: Note on family: A person's family will have an impact on our married life. This is a realistic expectation. Also, it harmful to falsely assume a person's family is perfect or can be "completing." This is not a realistic expectation.

FACILITATORS: Note on mental illness: sensitive topic, people in the group who either have struggled with mental health or have close family who struggle with mental health. This is wise to be upfront about with a potential partner, while it may not preclude someone from being a strong or healthy partner it is like any other important topic to discuss.)

2. What is the difference between being "defined" by something and being "impacted" by something? What is the role of Christ and redemption in determining which applies to us?

3. If anyone can enjoy a movie, a hike, coffee conversation, or making out, then how important is a person's spiritual growth when deciding on a relationship or marriage? How does this apply to me and my own spiritual growth?

4. Discuss the following verses from Proverbs and how they are important when evaluating "building a family.""

Proverbs 24:3-7 By wisdom a house is built, and by understanding it is established; by knowledge the rooms are filled with all precious and pleasant riches. A wise man is full of strength, and a man of knowledge enhances his might, for by wise guidance you can wage your war, and in abundance of counselors there is victory. Wisdom is too high for a fool; in the gate he does not open his mouth.

Proverbs 12:15 The way of a fool is right in his own eyes, but a wise man listens to advice.

Proverbs 29:11 A fool gives full vent to his spirit, but a wise man quietly holds it back.
