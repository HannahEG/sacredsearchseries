Chapter 1 Questions


Get to know you:
How do you personally deal with conflict, and in your opinion what is the ideal way conflict ought to be handled?

The following are just a few key Scriptures in this regard (there are many others).
- If someone sins against you (Matthew 18:15-20)
- Restoring someone in sin (Galatians 6:1-5)
- Forgive a sinner (Colossians 3:12-14)
- Reconciling differences (**Matthew 7:1-5,** Matthew 5:23-24)

Chapter 1 Discussion Questions:
1. First, have you ever asked yourself the question, "Why do I want to get married?" (if indeed you do).
How would getting married with Matthew 6:33 in mind change the way you pursue people and the type of person you might consider as you move forward?

2. How does Matthew 6:33 help establish a firm foundation for both a Biblical pursuit of marriage and marriage itself?
Why does the "Why" matter?
3. Tears of pain and tears of joy.
How does the example of marriage as a burden vs a blessing impact you? Does it surprise you that in marriage both kinds of tears are inevitable?

4. What does it take for selfish people to become servants? (Philippians 2:4-8)
Why is living for self a cause for misery? (James 3:16-17)

5. From some of the prominent ideas in this chapter (e.g. shared mission, no one can be enthralled by another person forever,
romantic infatuation/sexual attraction, tears of joy vs pain, "Why" vs "Who")
which stood out to you the most?
When can you take time to prayerfully ask the Lord about these concepts this week? (James 1:5)

*Time Permitting:*
6. Question from end of chapter "Study Questions" or other individual questions from chapter material.
